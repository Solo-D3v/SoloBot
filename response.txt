Santa Barbara

Santa Barbara is a city located on the California Central Coast in Santa Barbara County, California. It is located about 100 miles (160 km) northwest of Los Angeles and about 300 miles (480 km) south of San Francisco. It is the county seat and largest city of the county. The city has a population of about 91,364, making it the second most populous city in the county after Santa Maria. It is home to a number of tourist attractions, including the historic Spanish mission, the Santa Barbara Zoo, and many beaches. The University of California, Santa Barbara and the city of Santa Barbara are also important to the region, and the city is often referred to as the "American Riviera".
